Iconic Attractions

1. St. Mark's Basilica (Basilica di San Marco)

Description: A masterpiece of Byzantine architecture, St. Mark's Basilica is Venice's most famous church, adorned with stunning mosaics and housing the relics of St. Mark.

Must-See: The Pala d'Oro, an intricate gold altarpiece, and the panoramic view from the basilica's terrace.

2. The Grand Canal (Canal Grande)

Description: The Grand Canal is the main waterway of Venice, lined with magnificent palaces and bustling with gondolas, vaporettos, and water taxis.

Must-See: A gondola ride at sunset or a vaporetto ride from the Rialto Bridge to St. Markâ€™s Square.

3. Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale)

Description: Once the residence of the Doge, Venice's ruler, this Gothic palace is a symbol of Venice's political power and artistic heritage.

Must-See: The Golden Staircase, the Grand Council Chamber, and a walk across the Bridge of Sighs.

4. Rialto Bridge (Ponte di Rialto)

Description: The oldest and most iconic bridge spanning the Grand Canal, the Rialto Bridge offers stunning views and connects the bustling Rialto market with San Marco.

Must-See: The view of the Grand Canal from the bridge and a stroll through the nearby markets.
